🎯 Core Analytical Pillars
1. The Illusion of Competence
An investigation into how frictionless design tools (Canva, AI templates) create an aesthetic "professionalism" that can hide a lack of substantive technical understanding.

2. Digital Identity & Ethics
A debate on the risks of "Performance over Authenticity" on platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub, focusing on the concept of Digital Permanence and the ethics of professional branding.

3. The Accountability Gap
Evaluating the boundary between digital collaboration and academic dishonesty within shared workspaces like Google Docs and collaborative coding environments.

4. Behavioral Cybersecurity
An analysis of why "Awareness" often fails to prevent "Scams." This section explores the Urgency Hook and Scarcity Principle that attackers use to bypass logical thinking.

5. Professional Etiquette
A study on how informal digital communication habits (instant messaging) negatively impact high-stakes formal interactions with faculty and industry leaders.


🎓 Aerospace Engineering Perspective
As an Aerospace student, I argue that digital literacy is a safety requirement. In an industry where a "visual error" can lead to structural failure, I advocate for a "Zero-Trust Architecture" in personal digital habits and a transition from a Consumer of Convenience to a Critical Thinker.
